Neapolitan Classic Pizza Dough

Neapolitan pizza is a rather light dish, and the longer fermentation period and choice of ingredients make it a very digestible and tasteful choice!

For our more traditional dough, we will only use four ingredients

  • Water
  • Sea Salt
  • 0 or 00 flour (anything around 11,0% ~ 12,0% protein content should work early on!)
  • Fresh or dry yeast (fresh is the best choice)

For the preparation, a few important steps should be put into consideration - most importantly the temperature of the room; but also things like humidity, water temperature and storage also make the list.

Ingredients (for 7-8 pizzas)

  • 1 kilogram of flour (0 or 00 flour, as per Italian standards)
  • 600 miligrams of water
  • 30 grams of sea salt
  • 3 grams of fresh yeast (or 0.3 grams of dry yeast)

Steps

  1. Add all water content into a large container or bowl
  2. Dissolve the sea salt in water with your hands, until completely gone
  3. Dissolve the yeast into the water - due to the salt being present in the water, this process must be fast
  4. Quickly add some of the flour into the water and mix it well until liquid - this will prevent the yeast from dying due to the high salt content present
  5. Add flour little by little and mix with hands, incorporating air into the mixture with your fingers
  6. Once the mixture becomes more solid than liquid, start utilizing your closed hands until nearly all the flour is added
  7. When you feel it all come together and detach from the bowl, proceed to knead it into the counter or work surface you have available
  8. Once fully homogenous, let the dough rest for 15 to 30 minutes
  9. "Cut" the doughballs, an average of 250-260grams per, and properly store them into a container
  10. Keep dough raising for 12-16 hours at room temperature, with 25C being the highest desirable
  11. Stretch the dough and add ingredients before cooking in 400-450C oven for 90 seconds or so
  12. Voila!
